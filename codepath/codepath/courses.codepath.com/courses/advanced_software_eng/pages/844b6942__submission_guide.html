Submitting Coding Exercises

There are two types of weekly submissions in this course:

  1. Warmup Tasks - These are weekly required and curated leetcode questions that are due before the first session each week as a pre-class warmup. These are completed and submitted on LeetCode directly through your signed-in LeetCode account.
  2. HackerRank Assessment - These are weekly required HackerRank assessments which involve several multiple-choice or coding questions to be completed as part of each week. We will send your score to you each week to help you understand your progress at each point throughout the course.

In addition, there are a few special coding projects during the course that you'll be submitting through Github. Check that guide for a detailed review of those steps.

How to Submit Coding Exercises

We automatically scan your LeetCode account and HackerRanks so you don't have to do anything except submit directly on LeetCode and HackerRank through the links we provide and with your Github and email matching the ones associated with this course.

Sometimes our system can get confused if you use a different LeetCode account username or provide an alternate email for your HackerRank. To avoid any issues with your submissions:

  1. LeetCode has same username as your GitHub - Make sure you are have a LeetCode username that matches your Github username
  2. HackerRank has matching email - Make sure that when taking the HackerRank tests, you always provide the same email address there each time that you have registered with us for the course.

If your LeetCode username does not match your Github username, or you used an alternative email address on HackerRank, or you otherwise suspect that there may be issues or you aren't sure if your submissions are being recorded properly, please reach out to us via support@codepath.org so we can make sure everything is linked up properly on our system.

Leetcode Scanner: Our LeetCode scanner has a few quirks to be aware of based on limitations of their system. The way our scanner works is that once the class starts and we have your correct LeetCode username in our system, we can only check your last 20 completed challenges at any time. Every hour our scanner will check your profile and keep track of those last 20 challenges, and this accumulates in our system over time to record all of your submissions. This means you might need to re-submit certain challenges to make sure they are recorded, especially in the first week as we get your username plugged into the system.

How to Submit Special Projects

On certain weeks, you'll encounter special projects which help apply the concepts we learn into practical coding assignments. You'll be submitting these special assignments through Github and the process for doing that is explained in this Github submission guide.
