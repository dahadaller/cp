Advanced Software Engineering (SE103)

This 12-week course will help you ace technical interviewing and give you a preview of real-world challenges in industry. This course is designed to prepare you to solve challenging algorithm problems in real interviews at top companies. The course also includes lectures, interview practice, industry mentors, and advanced info sessions from senior engineers.

This course assumes you know the basics of data structures like linked lists, trees, and graphs and feel comfortable with easy to medium difficulty interview questions. This is designed to be a challenging course that introduces advanced techniques and patterns and helps in solving tougher algorithm problems often not covered much in typical university courses.

Course Structure

In this course, there are a few major elements to the program:

  • Pre-session Warmup - Watch videos and complete challenges before coming to the session to make sure you are warmed up.
  • In-Session Lectures - Learn directly from instructors walking through problems and explaining techniques and tips.
  • In-Session Exercises - Work directly with other students to practice with drills, activities and challenges.
  • At-home Practice - Before the final weekly assessment, we assign you more challenges you can use to practice.
  • Weekly Assessment - Each week, there's a 90-minute HackerRank test as a final assessment with multiple choice and coding. Check out the HackerRank Assessments FAQ for more details.

Topics Covered

Topics introduced are focused on technical interviewing and practical fundamentals of software development including:

  • Behavioral and general interviewing tips
  • Practical data structures
  • Implementing efficient algorithms

Over the 12-weeks, the following topics will be highlighted:

  • Core data structures: Hash Tables, Arrays, Linked Lists
  • Complex data structures: Binary Trees, Heaps
  • Object Oriented Design/Systems Design
  • Searches: Binary Search, Breadth First Search/Depth First Search
  • Sorting: Merge Sort and Quick Sort
  • Recursion and Combinations
  • Dynamic Programming
  • Greedy Algorithms

The course will also include a preview of real-world software engineering roles and challenges including:

  • Fireside Q&A chats and info sessions with professional engineers
  • Industry mentors helping with mock interviews
  • Learn what it's like in the day-to-day of different software engineering roles
  • What are the common challenges faced in your first full-time software role

Zoom Video Classroom

All remote sessions will take place in a live remote Zoom classroom environment with an instructor, mentors and students, the format of these sessions and how to be prepared is explained below:

  • Make sure to have your computer ready and with the courses system open in your browser, all the content you’ll need is available within the course system
  • Zoom classroom sessions are all live remote, the instructor will first present critical content and do walkthroughs (and you can chat questions along the way)
  • A large portion of the session takes place in breakout room activities where each of you will be grouped with a small number of other students
  • For all sessions, be sure to have your camera/audio enabled and find a space where you will be able to actively work through activities with the other students.

We design all of our programs to be highly interactive and hands-on. One of the most important aspects of this program is practicing the verbalizing/communication and steps to real phone screens and interviews, going beyond the coding, a lot of the time in sessions is dedicated to working through specific activities with other students in small groups. And we focus on making it as real-world as possible based on the tech screens and internship interviews at top tech companies and practicing those steps with actual problems you’d encounter in these interviews.

HackerRank Assessments

Each week during the course, you will be required to submit one HackerRank assessment at the end of the unit. This will be a timed 90-minute test, with a combination of multiple choice and multiple coding questions for you to solve.

The HackerRanks you are taking during the course approximate closely to how many real HackerRanks provided by companies work and we select common questions asked that many companies use for screens.

These HackerRanks are for you all to be able to log lots of practice, get your own scores, and become very familiar with HackerRank screens. The tests also allow us to collect data to understand how each student is doing in the program at any given point. If you’re in a lower percentile of the class, it’s probably a signal that you need to increase your study time for the future weeks.

Check out the HackerRank Assessments FAQ for more details on how this works and what to expect.

Three Strikes Policy

We are following a three strikes policy for missing deadlines in this course, so be sure to complete the required tasks each week by the deadlines. In particular:

  • Attend all sessions except for those where you've requested an excused absence
  • Complete weekly warm-up questions on LeetCode before the first session each week
  • Submit your weekly HackerRank by the deadline each week

Each time a deadline is missed, we issue a strike. Receiving three strikes is grounds for withdrawal from the course.

If you need to miss a session, you can request an excused absence from your course portal before the session begins. At the top right you will find Quick Actions, where you can select Request Excused Absence from the drop-down. You are allowed 3 excused absences over the duration of the course.
